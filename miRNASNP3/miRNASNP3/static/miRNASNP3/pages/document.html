miRNASNP-v3 data source and methods

Features in miRNASNP-v3

Since the variation number identified in human genome was tremendously increased in last five years, we updated our miRNASNP database to v3. To better understand associations of functional miRNA-related SNVs with phenotypes, we categorized disease-related variations (DRVs) with diseases/traits annotations. In this miRNASNP-v3, we analyzed the effects of 7,161,741 single nucleotide variations (SNVs) and 505,417 DRVs on 1,897 pre-miRNAs (2,630 mature miRNAs) and 3’UTRs of 18,152 genes. In addition to updating the number of SNVs identified on miRNA/genes, we also added four new features. Now, miRNASNP-v3 becomes a one-stop resource for miRNA-related SNVs research with the following functions.

Update compared with miRNASNP-v2:

  • SNPs in pre-miRNAs and their impacts on miRNAs.
  • Target gain/loss by SNPs in miRNA seed region and gene 3'UTRs.
  • Online tools to predict novel SNP effect on miRNA function.
  • Experimentally verified miRNA-target interaction.
  • Expression and correlation of miRNAs and genes.

New added to miRNASNP-v3:

  • DRVs (NHGRI GWAS catalog, ClinVar, COSMIC) in miRNAs and gene 3'UTRs.
  • DRVs impact on miRNA target gain/loss and secondary structure.
  • Gene enrichment of miRNA target gain/loss by variations in miRNA seed regions.
  • Correlation of drug sensitivity and miRNAs.

Data summary of variations identified on miRNAs and target binding sites

More than 689,966,720 SNPs, 21,338,716 COSMIC non-coding variations and 444,715 ClinVar variations were mapped to 2,652 mature miRNAs derived from 1,913 pre-miRNAs. We performed target gain/loss analysis for variations in miRNA seed region and 3'UTR (methods in Predict target gain/loss section). The seed region is defined as a 7bp nucleotide fragment at the 5'end of the mature miRNA between 2 and 8. Meanwhile, we predicted the impact of all the variations in pre-miRNA on secondary structure. Finally, 7,161,741 SNPs and 505,417 DRVs were identified on 1,897 pre-miRNAs (2,630 mature miRNAs) and 3’UTRs of 18,152 genes. The detailed statistics of these data integrated in miRNASNP-v3 are listed below.

Predict target gain/loss

We predict the target gain/loss effect of SNPs in miRNA seed regions. Firstly, for SNPs in miRNA seed region, we obtained miRNA wild sequence and SNP allele sequence. Then, we used two target prediction tools TargetScan and miRmap to predict their target sites, respectively. Faiza et al, 2019 reported that combination of these two tools have best performance prediction in human. Four categories of results are recorded as wild TargetScan site (WT), wild miRmap site (WM), SNP TargetScan site (ST) and SNP miRmap site (SM). If one miRNA's target gene shows in both WT and WM, but not in either ST or SM, we called that the miRNA lost this target gene. On the contrary, if one target shows in both ST and SM, but not in either WT or WM, we called that the miRNA gained one target gene.

We also predict the target gain/loss effect of SNPs in genes' 3'UTRs. For SNPs in one gene 3'UTR, we obtained wild type 3'UTR sequence and corresponding SNP 3'UTR sequence. Then, we used two target prediction tools (TargetScan and miRmap) to predict their target sites respectively. Four categories of results are recorded as wild TargetScan site (WT), wild miRmap site (WM), SNP TargetScan site (ST), SNP miRmap site (SM). If one target site shows in both WT and WM, but not in either ST or SM, we called that the gene lost this target site. On the contrary, if one target both show in ST and SM, but not in either WT or WM, we called that the gene gained one target site.

This method is also applied to analyze the effect of DRVs in miRNA seed regions and 3'UTRs on target gain/loss.

Disease-related variations (DRVs) identified on miRNA/3'UTR in miRNASNP-v3

In the "Disease" module, we integrated pathological information of 232,927 non-coding variations from COSMIC, disease information of 37,405 variations from ClinVar and disease/trait information of 235,085 SNPs from NHGRI GWAS catalog. We divided these variations into three categories based on their location (miRNA seed, pre-miRNA and gene 3'UTRs regions). For variations in miRNA seed regions and gene 3'UTRs, target gain/loss information was presented. For variations in pre-miRNAs, we provided the minimum free energy change of secondary structure of the pre-miRNA caused by the variation. In this module, users can screen for variations associated with a particular disease by selecting a tissue of interest or typing in a description of the disease.

To investigate SNPs with GWAS disease/traits, we downloaded all the tagSNPs from NHGRI GWAS catalog with the linkage disequilibrium (LD) information between tagSNPs and surrounding SNPs (D'>0.98, a LD measurement to show the degree of linkage disequilibrium between the selected variant and other variants) of 26 populations by the Ensembl REST API (http://rest.ensembl.org/documentation/info/ld_pairwise_get). Then, a genome block between the minimum and maximum coordination of the surrounded SNPs was defined as the GWAS identified trait associated regions of each tagSNP in each population. Finally, we identified 235,085 SNPs (including tagSNPs) in trait associated regions. For each SNP, a plot of GWAS identified trait associated regions was presented (Plot regions ±250kb around SNP position) in SNP detail panel.

miRNA gained/lost target gene set enrichment analysis

One SNV occurs on the miRNA seed region could lead to gain and/or lose a set of target genes. This gained/lost set of target genes could be enriched in special biological pathways, so that the SNV could affect the miRNA function. To further benefit users investigating biological functions of the altered miRNA targets caused by SNVs. We performed Gene Ontology (GO) and Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes (KEGG) enrichment analysis on gained/lost gene sets with gene numbers from 50 to 1000. We performed GO and KEGG enrichment analysis by an R-package clusterProfiler with default parameters. The cutoff of P-value and Q-value are set to 0.05. In general, 15.25% (950/6229) SNPs and 3.2% (19/592) COSMIC mutations in miRNA seed region have enrichment results. It should be noted that mutations sharing the same genomic coordinate and reference/allele with SNPs are not processed. The output of enrichment analysis will be presented in the Enrichment tag on miRNA detail page.

Correlation between miRNA expression and drug sensitivity

Drug sensitivity data (GI50) of 18,724 drug/compounds and 355 miRNAs expression data from NCI-60 were download from CellMinerCDB. The drug/compounds were annotated by NSC ID with its corresponding PubChem SID. Then we performed Pearson correlation analysis between drug sensitivity and miRNA expression. The result is present in miRNA detail panel, for hsa-miR-10b-5p example. And we only preserve miRNA-compound pairs with |cor|>0.5 and FDR<0.05 in NCI-60.

Correlation between miRNA and target gene in TCGA cancers

Gene expression data and miRNA expression data across 33 cancers were downloaded from The CanCer Genome Atlas (TCGA) portal. For each cancer, we performed pearson correlation analysis between genes and miRNAs, which can be easily obtained with GSCALite. The expression profiles and correlation details can be browsed in miRNA target gain/loss detail page. The overall correlation coefficient value was displayed in loss targets information panel, which can be clicked to pop out a histogram of correlation coefficient value for each cancer type (coefficient values were reserved only with P-value<0.05). In expression panel, a mean expression was provided, which can be clicked to see expression level of the gene/miRNA in each cancer type. However, because there is no expression data for SNP type 3'UTR/miRNA, the correlation coefficient value for gain target is not available.

Experimentally verified miRNA-target interactions (MTIs)

We integrated experimentally verified miRNA-target interactions (MTIs) from the databases of starBase, TarBase, miRTarBase, miRecords and miR2Disease. We downloaded 370,058 MTIs with supporting CLIP-seq (HITS-CLIP, PAR-CLIP, iCLIP, CLASH) experiments (>5) from starBase. 422,390 and 374,907 MTIs were collected from TarBase and miRTarBase respectively, they had been validated by luciferase report assay, western blot and high-throughput experiments. We also integrated 240 MTIs from miRecords experimentally verified by literature curation, and 641 MTIs from miR2Disease by luciferase reporter assay. Generally, 1,027,355 experimentally verified MTIs were obtained in miRNASNP-v3. It should be noted that, miRNA names were uniformed by miRBase nomenclature, and gene names was annotated by gene symbols.

Predict module

miRNASNP-v3 provides three kinds of online predictions. (1) Predicting the impact of variant in gene 3'UTR on miRNA:mRNA interaction. First, this method will scan 2,652 mature miRNAs to predict target miRNAs for wild-type and SNP-type 3'UTR sequence by miRmap and TargetScan. Then follow the analysis pipeline constructed in miRNASNP to get the gain/loss targets. (2) Predicting the impact of variant in miRNA on miRNA-target interaction, this method will scan 18,286 gene 3'UTRs to predict target genes for wild-type and SNP-type miRNA sequence.(In order to save the calculation time, only the longest transcript of each gene was used to take online prediction in this module.) The prediction result will give a summary panel and other four panels to show gain targets, loss targets, predicted targets of wild/SNP-type sequence. (3) For predicting the impact of variations in pre-miRNA on miRNA structure, the minimum free energy (MFE) of SNP-type sequence for each SNP is predicted by RNAfold.
